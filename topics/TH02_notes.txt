[Note ID: 9]
FIRST LAW OF THERMODYNAMICS:

STATEMENT: Energy is neither created nor destroyed, only converted between forms. Energy entering a system equals energy stored in or leaving it. E_in - E_out = Delta_E_system.

APPLICATION TO POWER SYSTEMS:
1. Heat Transfer: Heat input from energy source to working fluid
2. Fluid Conversion: Energy transfer to working fluid
3. Work Extraction: Expanding fluid performs mechanical work in turbine
4. Generator Conversion: Mechanical work converts to electrical energy
5. Heat Rejection: Low-pressure fluid exhausted to heat sink (condenser)
6. Pump Work: Fluid pressure increased for return to system

ENERGY BALANCE: Total energy in from heat source = Electrical power out + Heat rejected.
Efficiency = Electrical out / Total thermal in x 100%.

STORED vs. TRANSIENT ENERGY:

STORED ENERGY (energy a mass HAS): Potential Energy, Kinetic Energy, Internal Energy, Flow Energy

TRANSIENT ENERGY (energy IN TRANSIT): Heat (energy transfer due to temperature difference), Work (energy transfer due to force acting through distance)

SIGN CONVENTIONS:
• Heat added to system: Q positive, Heat removed: Q negative
• Work done BY system: W positive, Work done ON system: W negative

[Note ID: 10]
ENERGY FORMS IN THERMODYNAMIC SYSTEMS:

POTENTIAL ENERGY (PE): Due to position above reference point. Formula: PE = m x z x g / gc.

KINETIC ENERGY (KE): Due to motion/velocity. Formula: KE = m x v2 / (2 x gc).

INTERNAL ENERGY (U): Sum of all microscopic energy forms within substance. Specific internal energy u = U/m. Changes with temperature and pressure.

FLOW ENERGY (PV ENERGY): Energy a fluid has due to its pressure and volume. Specific flow energy = P x v. The work needed to push fluid into/out of system.

ENTHALPY (H): H = U + PV. Enthalpy = Internal Energy + Flow Work. Measure of a fluid's total energy content.

SPECIFIC ENTHALPY (h): h = u + Pv. Enthalpy per unit mass. Units: Btu/lbm. Used for all thermodynamic cycle calculations. Found in steam tables.

WHY ENTHALPY MATTERS:
• Directly represents energy available from working fluid
• Used to calculate turbine work: Wturbine = hin - hout
• Used to calculate pump work: Wpump = hout - hin
• Simplifies energy balance calculations for flowing systems
• Steam tables provide direct values

[Note ID: 11]
HEAT (Q):

DEFINITION: Transient energy transferred due to temperature difference. Flows from hotter to cooler. Spontaneous (no work required). NOT a property. Path-dependent.

WORK (W):

DEFINITION: Transient energy from force acting through distance. Formula: W = F x d. Units: Btu, ft-lbf, Joules. NOT a property (depends on process).

TWO TYPES:
1. Mechanical Work: Solid object moved by force. W = F x Delta_x.
2. Flow Work: Energy to push/pull fluid through pipes. P x V energy. Included in enthalpy.

TURBINE/PUMP WORK:
• Turbine: Input high-pressure steam, Output mechanical work + low-pressure steam. Work = hin - hout.
• Pump: Input low-pressure fluid + mechanical work, Output high-pressure fluid. Work = hout - hin.

POWER:

DEFINITION: Rate of doing work. Power = Work / Time. Units: Btu/s, Watts, horsepower. Conversion: 1 hp = 0.707 Btu/s = 746 Watts.

APPLICATIONS:
• Turbine power = specific work x mass flow rate
• Pump power = specific work x mass flow rate
• Generator power = turbine power - friction losses
